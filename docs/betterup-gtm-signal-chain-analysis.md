## Source: LinkedIn profile screen captures, April 13, 2026
## For use in BetterUp Revenue Signal Audit, Section 3

## The Reframe

This is not a CEO LinkedIn audit. It is a go-to-market signal chain assessment. The question is not "is Alexi posting well?" The question is: across the entire organization that touches the buyer (CEO, marketing leadership, sales leadership, account management, coach network), does BetterUp's conviction show up in a way that builds trust with enterprise buyers?

The answer, based on the data across six profiles, is no.

## The Five Signal Chain Failures

### 1. Nobody is posting buyer-facing content.
Across six profiles and 50+ posts reviewed, zero posts contain: specific enterprise ROI data, client outcome metrics with numbers, competitive differentiation claims, or CHRO-specific pain point content. The entire go-to-market team's LinkedIn presence is optimized for internal culture and event promotion, not for the buyer's consideration journey.

### 2. The marketing leader is less visible than the account managers.
Amy Spieth (SVP Marketing) has lower engagement than Willi Miller (Sr. AM). The person responsible for shaping market narrative is the least active voice in the signal chain. This means BetterUp's brand narrative on LinkedIn is effectively unmanaged.

### 3. Sales reps have no content strategy.
Jonathan Chang and Willi Miller are both experienced enterprise sellers. Neither has a content approach that positions them as a trusted advisor to their buyer. In a world where 70%+ of the buying journey happens before sales contact, these profiles are not building pre-sale trust.

### 4. Engagement is almost entirely internal.
Across all profiles, commenters are overwhelmingly: BetterUp employees, BetterUp coaches, personal network contacts, and L&D practitioners. Enterprise budget holders (CHRO, CLO, VP of Talent at 1,000+ employee companies) are virtually absent. The signal chain is a closed loop.

### 5. The company page does the heavy lifting, but nobody amplifies it strategically.
Amy L. Cole reposts company content regularly. The others share occasionally. But none of them add original commentary that would extend the reach or deepen the narrative. The company page's 241K followers are doing most of the work, and even that content skews toward awareness over consideration.

## What This Means for the Audit

The Section 3 reframe should present this as a "Go-to-Market Signal Chain" assessment, not just a CEO audit. The finding is not "Alexi needs to post better." The finding is: the conviction that drives BetterUp's product is not flowing through any part of the go-to-market organization on LinkedIn. The CEO posts philosophy. Marketing promotes events. Sales is invisible. Account management shares personal updates. And the buyer, conducting self-directed research, encounters a company with strong brand awareness and zero human proof points from the people who would actually serve them.

This is the Conviction Cascade, Layer 2 and Layer 4, made visible in LinkedIn data.
