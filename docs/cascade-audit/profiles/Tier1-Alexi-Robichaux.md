## Key Finding
Alexi has the conviction; the conviction never reaches the buyer because the platform he uses to express it is structured around the wrong audience. Across a 365-day window expanded from ninety days because his posting cadence is so low, seven of fourteen items are reposts of partner customers and BetterUp executives, and of the seven originals, one approaches the kind of operator-language proof a CHRO would put in front of her CFO; sixteen of his most recent visible comments are three-to-ten-word congratulations on people moving roles, and roughly a third of those are congratulations on former BetterUp employees taking jobs elsewhere, which is the same talent-exodus narrative the brand health assessment already flags as a Layer-4 cascade break, authored in real time by the CEO himself.

## Three Things That Would Move the Needle
1. **Convert the philosophy posts to operator language.** Every "human potential" post needs a paired post with an outcome a CHRO can quote in a CFO meeting (a ButcherBox-style story but with the metric in Alexi's own voice — "manager effectiveness up X%" or "ramp-time on new managers down Y weeks"). Aim for one outcome-anchored original post per month.
2. **Build a CHRO 100 list and treat substantive comments as the strategy, not the residue.** Ten outbound comments per week of fifty words or longer on CHROs', CLOs', and VPs of People's posts. Zero of them congratulating a departure.
3. **Stop the public departure-congratulations pattern.** Eight of sixteen recent comments congratulate former BetterUp employees on landing at IBM, BCG, Workday, Salesforce, Spreha, ServiceNow, Otter, and Quantum. Each one is a publicly indexed signal that BetterUp leaders leave. Move the warmth to private channels and replace the public-comment slot with engagement on a buyer-side post.
