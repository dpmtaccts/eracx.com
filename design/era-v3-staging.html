What if the problem is not that your pipeline is too small, but that 95% of the accounts in it do not know you, do not trust you, and are not ready to buy from anyone?

At any given moment, only five in every hundred accounts in your market are in-market. The other ninety-five are not your enemy. They are your inventory.

Funnel thinking wastes them. It calls them unqualified and moves on. Warmth thinking treats them as the asset that matures. Every signal captured, every loop run, every campaign deployed is a deposit.

ERA designs the deposit system. What to listen for. How to compound it. When to draw down.

§03   Point of view

Five things
we believe.

We build AI-powered ABM programs that generate warmth with your highest-value accounts. Here is why we do it the way we do.

Principle 01

Only 3–5% are
actively buying.

Of every 100 ideal customers, three to five are in-market right now. The rest are not unqualified. They are not yet.

What we do Treat the 95% as the asset. Compound warmth while competitors chase the 5%.
Principle 02

Nobody
buys alone.

Every buyer has someone to answer to. The average B2B buying committee has 6 to 10 people. Solo decisions are a myth.

What we do Multi-thread by default. Champion plus economic buyer plus one adjacent stakeholder, minimum.
Principle 03

Decisions happen
earlier than you think.

By the time a buyer fills out your demo form, 60 to 80% of the decision is already made. They came to confirm a choice, not to evaluate one.

What we do Instrument the months before the form. Dark activity, content engagement, peer mentions.
Principle 04

Don't pitch
strangers.

Cold outreach asks for a meeting before asking for a reason. We pitch when warmth supports it. Never before.

What we do Outreach fires when warmth crosses a defined threshold. Every message is earned by a prior signal.
Principle 05

Not all customers
are equal.

The top 20% of named accounts drive most of the revenue. Headcount math is seductive and wrong. Deepen the right accounts; don't widen the wrong funnel.

What we do Design around 50 to 250 named accounts. Fewer accounts, higher warmth, better revenue.
