§ 01 / Method
What we did

Four waves of work, not one pass.

Enrichment is not a single button. The reliability of a contact record depends on how many sources agreed, whether the email actually reaches a human, and whether the profile matches reality as of today.

01

Started with HubSpot, audited first.

Exported the full CRM, audited for structural problems, normalized fields, and flagged records that were obviously broken or stale. No enrichment happens until the base file is clean enough to match against. Garbage in, garbage enriched.

02

Layered enrichment across verified sources.

Every record went through multiple B2B intelligence providers that aggregate from different places: public professional profiles, company databases, verified business directories, and crowdsourced contact graphs. When the sources agreed on a field, we trusted it. When they disagreed, we stored both values and flagged the record for a later pass.

03

Every email verified before it counts.

A populated email field is not the same as a deliverable one. Catch-all domains, role-based aliases, and long-departed employees all produce technically valid records that bounce. Sending to those addresses burns sender reputation, and a burned sender hurts every campaign that follows. Every address on the reachable list has been verified.

04

Contained in waves, not one pass.

Enrichment tools disagree with each other, and sometimes they disagree with reality. We processed the file in waves, re-matching and re-cleaning between passes, which is how the numbers on this page became stable enough to put in front of you.

Methodology and definitions
  • Source file: Enriched Navalent workbook, 5,566 rows. HubSpot export is the base record; a multi-provider enrichment pass provides the current-state values.
  • Fully enriched records: Rows where enrichment returned a non-empty title or company name. 4,170 of 5,566 (74.9%).
  • Likely buyers: Classified from the current enriched title using a rule-based match against Chief/C-suite, President, Founder/Owner, Managing Director, Managing Partner, VP/SVP/EVP, Head of, and senior People/Talent/HR leadership. Excludes IC "business partner" roles (HRBP, strategic partner, etc.) to avoid overcounting.
  • Hidden buyers: Records whose HubSpot title did not meet the buyer definition but whose enriched title does. 1,256 records. This is the segment most likely to produce warm outbound, because the contact existed in Navalent's database but was not previously flagged as a target.
  • Company changed: Normalized HubSpot company differs from normalized enriched company name. Normalization strips whitespace, case, and punctuation. 1,439 records total; 303 of those hold buyer titles and have a verified email.
  • Reachable: Has a populated and verified email address. Verification happens at the SMTP layer, not just field-level presence.
  • Not included yet: Explicit "changed jobs in the last N days" flag, intent data, prior engagement signals. Those belong in the next pass.
